Help – Universal Plotting Sheet

What it Does

This web site is a digital universal plotting sheet for plotting lines of position from sight reductions. This help page assumes you know:

  • what a sight reduction is
  • how to set up a real (paper) universal plotting sheet
  • and how to find a, T/A, Zn, a-Lat, and a-Lon.

How it Works

Once you have your work form filled out, set up the sheet's central latitude and longitude by entering them into the fields at the top left. Then enter your a, T/A, Zn, a-Lat, and a-Lon into the fields at the top right. Click “Add LOP” to add the line of position to the sheet.

Repeat this process for as many sights as you like. When two lines of position intersect, the sheet will draw a circle around the intersection. To see the latitude and longitude of the intersection, simply hover your mouse over the circle. The coordinates will be displayed at the bottom left.

Entering Values

Fields that take degrees and/or minutes will accept a variety of formats. For example, to enter 37° N latitude, you can type any of the following:

  • 37N
  • 37*N
  • +37
  • 37
  • 37° N

In other words, you can leave out the degree sign, add or remove spaces, and use +/- instead of N/S. You can also use an asterisk * instead of a degree sign.

When entering minutes—in the a field, for instance—you can leave off the tenths. You can also leave off the ’ mark. For example, to enter 3.0’, you can type any of the following:

  • 3
  • 3’
  • 03'
  • 3.0'

Finally, when entering degrees and minutes in the a-Lon field, you can use any combination of the formats above. For example, to enter 118° 04.7’ W, you can type any of the following:

  • -118 4.7
  • 118 4.7 W
  • 118*4.7'W
  • 118° 04.7 W
  • 118° 04.7’ W
